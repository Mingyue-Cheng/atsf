01 — Abstract

A Paradigm Shift in
Time Series Forecasting

TL;DR

We argue that forecasting should move beyond static, model-centric pipelines toward adaptive agentic systems that perceive, plan, act, reflect, and remember — evolving continuously through interaction and accumulated experience.

Conventional time series forecasting treats prediction as a model-centric, static, and single-pass problem. This framing proves fundamentally inadequate for complex, adaptive, multi-turn scenarios encountered in real-world deployments. We propose Agentic Time Series Forecasting (ATSF) — a principled framework that organizes forecasting as an iterative agentic workflow. Rather than optimizing a single model’s predictive accuracy in isolation, ATSF enables forecasting systems to interact with tools, incorporate feedback from outcomes, and evolve through accumulated experience. We formalize the framework through five interconnected cognitive components — Perception, Planning, Action, Reflection, and Memory — and propose three concrete implementation paradigms: workflow-based design, agentic reinforcement learning, and hybrid agentic workflows. We conclude by outlining key research opportunities and open challenges to guide the community toward this new frontier.

02 — Framework

Five Cognitive Components
of ATSF

👁

Perception

Extracts task-relevant information from raw, noisy inputs. Adaptive preprocessing that determines what information matters for specific forecasting contexts — not fixed, but situationally aware.

🧠

Planning

Formulates forecasting objectives and decomposes complex tasks into manageable subtasks. Crucially, planning is dynamic — revised continuously based on new observations or feedback.

⚡

Action

Executes decisions through autonomous tool interaction. Treats forecasting itself as one action within a broader action space, enabling flexible integration of diverse methods and external APIs.

🔍

Reflection

Enables iterative correction by evaluating, interpreting, and revising predictions. Supports self-judgment without external supervision — the system critiques its own outputs.

💾

Memory

Preserves reusable experience — patterns, strategies, failure cases — across instances. Hierarchical access at instance, task, and domain levels enables continual learning.

04 — Implementation

Three Implementation
Paradigms

PARADIGM · 01
Workflow-Based Design
Structured Cognitive Pipelines
Structures forecasting as predefined sequences of cognitive steps using directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) or standard operating procedures. Organizes perception, planning, action, and reflection into interpretable, deterministic pipelines that are easy to audit and debug.
✓ High Interpretability ✓ Stability ⚠ Rigid for Novel Scenarios
PARADIGM · 02
Agentic RL
AgenticRL — Autonomous Evolution
Applies reinforcement learning to the decisions surrounding forecasting — objective planning, action selection, strategy revision — rather than predictive models alone. Enables autonomous discovery of novel forecasting strategies beyond human-designed heuristics.
✓ Autonomous Evolution ✓ Novel Discovery ⚠ Training Instability
PARADIGM · 03
Hybrid Agentic Workflow
AgentFlow — Best of Both Worlds
Integrates explicit workflow design with localized reinforcement learning at specific decision points. Balances the stability and interpretability of structured workflows with the adaptability of learned strategies, enabling controlled evolution within safe boundaries.
✓ Balanced Design ✓ Adaptable ⚠ Architectural Complexity
